**5. Training Frequency & Weight Gain - Correlation**

**6. Changes to Improve Hypertrophy and Weight Gain**

Here’s a prioritized list of changes, considering your data:

1.  **Increase Volume:** Increase the volume of each exercise by 10-20%.  Focus on making these exercises longer and incorporating more repetitions.
2.  **Progressive Overload:** Implement a consistent program of gradually increasing the weight lifted over time. Even small increases (2.5-5 lbs) per workout significantly impact hypertrophy.
3.  **Tempo & Rep Range:** Implement a 3-5 second eccentric (lowering) phase in many exercises.  This promotes more muscle fiber recruitment.
4.  **Rest Intervals:** Reduce rest times between sets. Longer rest intervals between sets will increase metabolic stress and recovery time, facilitating hypertrophy.
5.  **More Complex Exercises:** Swap some of the "simple" exercises for more complex ones.  For example, move to a Front Squat instead of Bulgarian Split Squat if you’re focused on quad development.
6.  **Consider a Drop Set:**  At the end of each set, reduce the weight by 20-30% and perform another set. This adds a significant metabolic stimulus.
7.  **Track Progress – Crucial:** Don’t just look at the numbers.  Monitor your strength, rep ranges, and how you feel (overall fatigue). Adjust your training as needed.
